# Worked acceptance outcomes

These examples show how the same method can end in completion, rejection,
blockage, or a bounded scope decision. They are intentionally concrete because
most acceptance failures occur when an attractive narrative is allowed to
replace the pre-agreed rule.

## Valid completion

An agentic delivery system accepts a change when an independently owned
verifier confirms that the artifact belongs to the final integrated revision
and that a preserved invalid artifact is rejected.

Before intervention, an artifact from an earlier revision can be attached to a
later change and the pipeline reports success. The operator preserves that
artifact as the bad fixture and preserves a current valid artifact as the
valid-path fixture. The intervention binds evidence identity to the final
revision and moves verifier configuration outside the producer’s authority.

The same stale artifact passes before and fails after. The valid current
artifact passes after. Attempts to alter or bypass the verifier require
independent approval and remain visible. Evidence records identify the
revisions, procedures, raw receipts, collectors, reviewers, and hashes.

This is valid completion because the demonstrated result matches the written
accept and reject conditions, the comparison is like-for-like, and the
producer cannot approve its own exception. The sign-off still limits the
conclusion to the observed repositories, pipeline, tasks, and failure mode.

## Invalid proxy completion

A qualification requires five complete Rust quality gates to overlap while
performing no warm compiler or linker work. The producer reports five
successful gates and a good elapsed time. All commands eventually returned
success.

The operating-system evidence shows that a two-process admission cap produced
three serialized waves. The workload was narrower than the agreed command, and
cacheable compiler actions still ran. Timing and command success are genuine,
but they are cheaper proxies for the required simultaneous execution and
absence of warm work.

This is not “mostly passed.” The overlap and zero-warm-work criteria fail. The
operator preserves the result, records the mismatches, repairs any verifier
defect, and reruns the original rule. Reinterpreting “five completed” as “five
simultaneous,” deleting the failed evidence, or weakening the requirement
after seeing the result would be acceptance theatre.

## Legitimate blockage

A codebase criterion requires observing retry and duplicate-delivery behavior
against the production payment provider’s sandbox. The client owns the
credentials and account configuration. Local mocks can exercise the call site
but cannot reproduce the provider’s idempotency and webhook behavior.

The operator records the failed authentication procedure and immutable error
evidence, opens the agreed access request, follows up with the technical owner,
and escalates to the decision owner after the recorded response window. The
account remains unavailable. The independent reviewer confirms that the
pre-agreed escalation rule was satisfied and that the proposed mock is not
equivalent evidence.

The retry-safety criterion is marked `blocked`, not passed or not applicable.
Other independent criteria may retain their own statuses, but the final
decision states that production-provider retry safety remains unproven and
names the migration or release consequence. The blocked record identifies the
action, owner, rerun procedure, and stale evidence that must be recollected
when access is restored.

## Attempted scope expansion

A review scoped to payment idempotency reveals that a separate administrative
token-rotation path lacks an automated expiry check. The observation is
credible, but it is outside the named payment claims, target path, and agreed
gate.

The operator first asks whether the token issue can invalidate the evidence
already collected. If the payment evidence depended on the affected token
path, the relevant criterion is paused and the limitation is recorded. If it
does not, the observation is preserved as an out-of-scope referral with its
known consequence and uncertainty.

The new issue does not silently become another required intervention, and it
is not deleted. Expanding the work requires a written amendment naming the new
claim, target, evidence, participants, dependencies, and effect on existing
criteria. Likewise, the discovery cannot be used to claim that the original
payment criterion failed unless it actually undermines that criterion’s
evidence.

This keeps both sides honest: a new risk cannot be hidden to protect a clean
report, and an interesting discovery cannot create an unbounded obligation.
